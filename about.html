Rahul Jetwani

A Computer Science student and game programmer from Indore, M.P., who genuinely loves both playing games and building them. My journey into game development began naturally from curiosity. I’ve always enjoyed understanding how things work, and when I finally got my own computer around 3–4 years ago, that curiosity turned into hands-on creation. Since then, game development has become the craft I keep returning to every day.

For me, games are like films but better. They let you interact, explore, retry, and feel a part of the world rather than just watching it. I especially love multiplayer games because they are the best way to spend time and create memories with friends. Fast-paced FPS titles like Counter-Strike, Call of Duty, and Battlefield are my go-to, but story-driven experiences also hold a special place in my heart are titles like Resident Evil, Detroit: Become Human, and GTA Vice City shaped how I think about pacing, world-building, and game feel.

As a solo developer, I usually handle most parts of development myself especially gameplay systems, which is where I feel most comfortable and excited. While I primarily focus on gameplay programming, I’ve explored areas like UI, VFX, environment setup, and general game design because it helps me understand end-to-end development. Recently, I’ve also become more interested in engine programming and shader programming newer interests that I look forward to developing further.

I mainly work in Unity (2D & 3D) using C#, and it has become my favorite tool because of its flexibility, fast workflow, and ability to build games for anything like mobile, low-end devices, and high-end PCs. I also have hands-on experience with Unreal Engine (Blueprints), and while I know C++, I haven’t used it deeply within Unreal. Beyond engines, I’ve experimented with frameworks like Pygame and SFML, but Unity remains the place where I build most comfortably.

Most of the games I’ve completed were made under some deadline like Taxi Chaos, built for a college project, or Eyes on Duty, created to practice mobile-focused optimization and UI logic. I love participating in game jams such as BYOG and GMTK. Many of my jam projects don’t get completed on time, usually because I aim too big or exams get in the way but every jam teaches me valuable lessons in prototyping, scoping, and decision-making.

I learned Unity and C# on my own through YouTube and Udemy, completed a Diploma in Computer Science, and am currently pursuing my B.Tech in CSE. I’m still exploring and improving every day but if there’s one thing I’m sure of, it’s that building games is exactly where I want to be.
